Outside the Temple of Colors

When I decided to make this site for the Greetings from JRL-app, I also decided to make it in the form of an ASP.NET MVC with a Angular client.

In it's first incarnation it would literally be a single page app, so not much use for Angular for that, but I had a vague idea about making the app into a web-version, using the back end and Angular.

Not that I had any experience with Angular, but had recently done an MVA Learning Path about MEAN Stack Development, and I found the Angular-part most interesting: using data-binding and TypeScript it seemed it could be a web-frontend platform that I would actually enjoy working on.

So, I did end up making the web version: I just finished it, in fact. And I found out that I actually do like working with Angular & TypeScript!

This was Angular 5, and the MVA was actually for AngularJS, so the learning curve had some bumps. But the Tour of Heroes helped me catch up, and the Temple was finished within a couple of part-time weeks.

Hence, you can now also go on the quest for colors in the Temple of Colors, and at the same time learn more about me, right here on the web
